Klaus didn't come for the fitness.

He came because his Monday was empty. Because his office identity had evaporated three months into retirement. Because the guys he'd worked with for 30 years suddenly had no reason to text him anymore.

Six weeks in, someone noticed he wasn't there one Tuesday.

Not the instructor. The group.

"Where were you?" they asked the next week. And something shifted in him.

That question — asked by people who had nothing to gain from him showing up — mattered more than any workout ever could.

Here's what nobody tells you: after 60, your body doesn't need optimizing. It needs *companionship*.

A fitness studio counts reps. We count Tuesdays you wouldn't cancel for anything.

A program ends. A community carries you.

**You are a Mover.** Not someone who attends. Someone who *belongs*.

Your first session is free. Your spot is already waiting.
