# Social Media Post: Starting Something New at 60

---

**Platform:** Instagram / Facebook  
**Length:** 115 words  
**Format:** Image + Caption

---

**IMAGE:** Black-and-white photograph of a woman in her early 60s, hands on hips, mid-breath, in a group of others (blurred, community visible). Natural light. Real, not staged.

---

**CAPTION:**

Klaus turned 60 last month.

For the first time in 40 years, nobody needed him to be anything. No job title. No role.

He thought he'd miss that. Instead, he discovered something: he was curious about himself again.

Now there's a Tuesday. Eight people. The same eight people. They know when he's not there.

Last week, Klaus led the opening. Not because anyone asked. Because that's who he is in that room now.

You don't have to wait until you know who you are next.

You become that person by showing up.

Your first session is free. Your spot is already waiting.

---

**Why this works:**
- Opens with a specific, named person in a real moment (age, transition, loss of identity)
- Uses identity language throughout ("who he is now," "your spot")
- Shows the belonging mechanic (eight people, they notice his absence) without explaining it
- Addresses the cultural moment: post-retirement identity loss is real and underserved
- The opening ritual ("Klaus led the opening") demonstrates difference subtly
- Closes on transformation through belonging, not through fitness outcomes
- Tone is direct, slightly literary ("He was curious about himself again"), warm
