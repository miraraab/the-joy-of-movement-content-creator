# The Joy of Movement – Product Specifications

## Core Offering Philosophy
Joy of Movement is not a course provider. It is identity infrastructure
for people 55+ navigating life transitions. Movement is the vehicle.
Belonging is the product.

## Membership Tiers

### Tier 1 – Open Movement (Free / Low-Barrier)
- Open group sessions in parks or public spaces
- No commitment, no registration
- Purpose: entry point, trust-building, brand visibility
- Target: first-timers, skeptics, word-of-mouth referrals

### Tier 2 – Base Membership (15–25€/month)
- Fixed group, fixed day, fixed location
- Consistent group of 10–15 people
- Access to community platform
- Shared activities beyond movement sessions
- Purpose: identity formation through rhythm and belonging

### Tier 3 – Premium Membership (40–60€/month)
- Smaller groups (6–8 people)
- Special formats and experiences
- Priority access and discount on retreats
- Closer relationship with group leader
- Purpose: depth, margin, high retention

### Tier 4 – Annual Retreat (600–1,500€/year)
- The tribal ritual – the year's highlight
- Multi-day immersive experience
- Community builds toward this together
- Purpose: peak emotional bonding, highest margin

## §20 Health Insurance Integration
- Eligible prevention courses (§20 SGB V) serve as entry point
- Health insurance co-payment covers initial contact
- Strategy: §20 brings people in – identity keeps them for years
- Not the core product; the gateway product

## Group Format
- Sessions open with a fixed Opening Ritual (non-negotiable)
- Milestones celebrated publicly: 1 month, 6 months, 1 year
- Every new member shares their story (culture, not marketing)
- Member designation: not "Teilnehmer:in" – to be defined organically
  with first pilot group

## Geographic Scope
- Germany (DACH expansion later)
- Hyperlocal launch: one city, one group, 12 people
- Scalability via local group leaders, not centralized expansion

## Content Types Produced
- Blog posts (thought leadership, member stories)
- Social media posts (Instagram, Facebook – primary channels for 55+)
- Community updates and newsletters
- Retreat announcements and invitations
